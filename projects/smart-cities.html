Smart Cities Pandemic Policy Evaluation - India

View Full Paper

1. Policy Background

The Smart Cities Mission is an urban renewal initiative by the Indian government aimed at improving infrastructure, sustainability, and quality of life through technology. During the COVID-19 pandemic, 45 cities repurposed their Integrated Control and Command Centres into COVID-19 war rooms for monitoring, tracing, telemedicine, and service coordination.

2. Literature Review

  • Mullick et al. (2022): Highlights the digital divide in smart cities and the exclusion risks for marginalized populations.
  • Deloitte (2020): Reviews case studies of Indian smart cities' pandemic response strategies.

3. Research Design

This study uses a cross-sectional design comparing 100 smart cities with 100 non-smart cities to measure whether smart infrastructure affected case and death outcomes during the pandemic.

4. Outcome Variables

  • COVID-19 death rates
  • Total positive cases

5. Control Variables

  • Smart City selection score (2015)
  • Income, population density, and government funding
  • Education, vaccine coverage, age, and gender
  • Lockdown, distancing, and mask policies

6. Main Regression Equation

The core evaluation uses a difference-in-differences framework to estimate how smart-city infrastructure changed public-health outcomes.

Y_i = beta_0 + beta_1 SmartCity_i + sigma_c + epsilon_i

Here, sigma_c represents the vector of control variables listed above.

7. Additional Analyses

  • Sensitivity analysis and bandwidth checks
  • Cost-benefit analysis of smart infrastructure spending
  • Time-series comparisons of infection and response trends

8. Policy Discussion

If the treatment effect is significant, the results support further investment in smart public-health infrastructure. If the effect is weak or mixed, the implication is refinement rather than abandonment of the current intervention model.
